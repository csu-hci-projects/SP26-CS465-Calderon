## Formatting and Submission

- Use the CHI/ACM template from `acmart-primary.zip`.
- Use `\documentclass[manuscript]{acmart}` to make the paper one column.
- Upload both the final PDF and the zip.
- Canvas instructions supersede the course webpage if they conflict.

## Paper Length

- Minimum: 8-10 pages per group member.
- For a solo project, target at least 8 pages.
- AirDesk should likely aim for 8-10 pages including references if allowed, or 8-10 pages before references if the teacher clarifies that.

## Study Requirement

Only one study is required. The rubric examples:

- quantitative: 12 subjects;
- qualitative: thematic analysis with semi-structured interviews, 6 participants.

For AirDesk, the practical plan is Caden plus one roommate. The paper should acknowledge this as a small formative pilot and rely on the system/prototype contribution, logs, and honest limitations.

## Research Paper Breakdown

Total: 70 points.

- Overall paper: 25
- State of the art: 20
- Methods and evaluations: 20
- Prototype description and reproducibility: 5
- Limitations, future work, and conclusion: 5

## AirDesk Strategy

The paper should be strong where the rubric rewards it:

- State of the art: connect mid-air interaction, SIIDs, ability-based design, and hand tracking.
- Methods/evaluation: describe the small pilot plainly, then add objective prototype logs and replay evaluation.
- Prototype/reproducibility: include commands, architecture, safe dry-run behavior, and data artifacts.
- Limitations: be unusually clear that this is formative pilot evidence, not broad validation.
